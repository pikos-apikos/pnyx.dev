The machine of democracy

The Civic Loop

A repeating public process—not a feed, not a black box, not a sovereign machine.

  1. 01

    Raise the issue

    A citizen or community states the public problem, its scope, and its provenance.

  2. 02

    Assemble skills

    Human experts, language models, and hybrid teams take explicit analytical roles.

  3. 03

    Map the evidence

    Claims, sources, uncertainties, costs, rights, and capture risks remain visible.

  4. 04

    Deliberate publicly

    Society challenges, improves, rejects, or amends the proposal.

  5. 05

    Decide and act

    The final judgment belongs to citizens; execution remains constrained and accountable.

  6. 06

    Remember outcomes

    What happened becomes auditable public memory, ready to improve the next decision.

society→Pnyx→public artifact→public memory→better citizens & models→society

Design constraints

What must remain true

01

Judgment is not delegated to the model.

Machines can gather, compare, critique, and remember. Political sovereignty remains human.

02

Disagreement is part of the artifact.

Minority positions, uncertainty, rejected warnings, and real losses are not erased by synthesis.

03

The rules pass through the same loop.

Pnyx must expose its own classifications, failures, incentives, and capture attempts to public review.

04

Public work becomes public memory.

Evidence, software, standards, outcomes, and lessons funded by society return to society.
